  <section class="card" id="essentials">
    <h2>🏯 First Time Essentials</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>The Grand Palace &amp; Wat Phra Kaew (Emerald Buddha)</strong> – Bangkok’s most iconic landmark, dazzling architecture and the revered Emerald Buddha.</li>
      <li><strong>Wat Pho – the Reclining Buddha</strong> – Giant Reclining Buddha; birthplace of traditional Thai massage.</li>
      <li><strong>Wat Arun – the Temple of Dawn</strong> – Riverside temple with porcelain mosaics, stunning at sunrise/sunset.</li>
      <li><strong>Chatuchak Weekend Market</strong> – Huge market: street food to fashion.</li>
      <li><strong>Chinatown (Yaowarat Road)</strong> – Bustling streets, neon lights, top street food.</li>
      <li><strong>Rooftop Bars</strong> (Tichuca, Octave Marriott, Scarlett) – Sunset cocktails with sweeping views.</li>
      <li><strong>Wat Ratchanatdaram (Loha Prasat – Iron Castle)</strong> – Unique multi-tiered temple with dozens of spires.</li>
      <li><strong>Wat Benchamabophit – Marble Temple</strong> – Elegant white marble; best in morning light.</li>
      <li><strong>Erawan Museum</strong> – Iconic three-headed elephant building with Thai art.</li>
      <li><strong>Floating Markets</strong> (Damnoen Saduak, Amphawa) – Boats, canals, vibrant morning food scene.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="card" id="dosdonts">
    <h2>🙏 Do &amp; Don’t for Cultural Highlights</h2>
    <p class="lead">Dress &amp; temple etiquette</p>
    <ol>
      <li><strong>Do:</strong> Cover shoulders/knees; light fabrics (cotton/linen); bring a scarf; slip-on shoes; dress neatly.</li>
      <li><strong>Don’t:</strong> No sleeveless tops; no short shorts/mini-skirts; avoid ripped/flashy clothes; no hats/sunglasses inside; no beachwear.</li>
    </ol>
    <hr class="sep"/>
    <p class="small">Tip: keep it simple, clean and respectful — it goes a long way.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card" id="cultural">
    <h2>🎭 Cultural Spots (off the beaten path)</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>MOCA – Museum of Contemporary Art</strong> – Thai masters &amp; new talent.</li>
      <li><strong>Bangkok Art and Culture Centre (BACC)</strong> – Free-entry hub near Siam.</li>
      <li><strong>Jim Thompson House Museum</strong> – Teak house + Thai art.</li>
      <li><strong>Thailand Cultural Centre</strong> – Concerts, dance, opera, festivals.</li>
      <li><strong>Rattanakosin Exhibition Hall</strong> – Bangkok’s history &amp; kings.</li>
      <li><strong>Queen Sirikit Museum of Textiles</strong> – Inside Grand Palace.</li>
      <li><strong>Bangkokian Museum (Folk Museum)</strong> – 20th-century city life.</li>
      <li><strong>Artist’s House (Baan Silapin)</strong> – Canal-side puppetry &amp; crafts.</li>
      <li><strong>The National Gallery</strong> – Classical art &amp; rotating exhibits.</li>
      <li><strong>H Gallery</strong> – Indie contemporary in a mansion.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="card" id="scams">
    <h2>🚫 Don’t Get Fooled</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Tuk-tuk “cheap tour” detours.</li>
      <li>“Temple is closed” redirect scam.</li>
      <li>Non-meter taxis → insist on meter / Grab / Bolt.</li>
      <li>Gem &amp; tailor shop traps.</li>
      <li>Ping Pong show touts → hidden bills.</li>
      <li>Fake monks selling amulets.</li>
      <li>Jet ski “damage” fees.</li>
      <li>Tourist pricing → watch locals, haggle politely.</li>
      <li>ATM DCC trap → choose “no conversion”.</li>
      <li>Massage upsells → read menus, clear pricing.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="card" id="smart">
    <h2>💡 Smart Tips</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Use Grab/Bolt for clear pricing.</li>
      <li>Check temple hours online.</li>
      <li>Agree tuk-tuk fares upfront.</li>
      <li>Choose reputable massage shops.</li>
      <li>Bargain politely (smile!).</li>
      <li>Prefer big bank ATMs (KBank, SCB, BBL).</li>
      <li>Know fair prices (ask locals / check online).</li>
      <li>Ignore unsolicited street “deals”.</li>
      <li>Book tours via trusted operators.</li>
      <li>Trust your instincts.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="card" id="practical">
    <h2>📝 Practical Tips</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Stay hydrated (bottled water; no tap water).</li>
      <li>Use sunscreen (SPF &gt; 25, reapply).</li>
      <li>Beat traffic: BTS/MRT.</li>
      <li>Get connected: eSIM (AIS/True).</li>
      <li>Offline maps in Google Maps.</li>
      <li>ATM fees ≈ 250 THB; decline conversion.</li>
      <li>Getting around: Grab app.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
